# Question: Do spindle-memory effects hold in older adults where spindle activity naturally declines?

## Why This Matters

Sleep spindle density decreases substantially with aging, and age-related memory decline is a major public health concern. If spindles causally support memory consolidation, then the age-related decline in spindles could partly explain age-related memory decline — and spindle enhancement could be a therapeutic target. However, both current wiki sources used exclusively young adult samples, leaving this critical question unanswered.

## What We Know So Far

- [[claims/claim-spindle-density-predicts-consolidation]] — established in young adults (age 20-40)
- [[claims/claim-spindle-enhancement-improves-memory]] — demonstrated in young adults (age 22-40)
- Spindle density is known to decline with age (not yet sourced in this wiki)

## What Would Constitute an Answer

- An RCT of closed-loop spindle stimulation in older adults (60+) with memory outcomes
- A large observational study correlating spindle density with memory performance across the adult lifespan
- A study in MCI or early Alzheimer's patients testing spindle enhancement

## Related Questions

- Does spindle enhancement have different effects in populations with already-low baseline spindle density?
- Could spindle stimulation serve as a therapeutic intervention for age-related cognitive decline?

## Details

### 1. Does closed-loop spindle stimulation improve memory?

**Rodriguez (2024)** found a significant memory benefit of spindle stimulation (d=0.68, p=0.003). **Kumar (2025)** failed to replicate this in a larger, pre-registered, multi-site study (d=0.18, p=0.22, BF01=4.2).

The physiological effect (spindle enhancement) is consistent across both studies. The disagreement is specifically about the downstream cognitive benefit.

Possible resolution: the memory effect may be moderated by baseline spindle density (Kumar's exploratory analysis found d=0.52 in low-baseline participants vs d=-0.06 in high-baseline).

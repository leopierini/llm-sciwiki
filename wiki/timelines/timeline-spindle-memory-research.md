# Timeline: Sleep Spindles and Memory Research

Chronological evolution of evidence on the spindle-memory link, as tracked by this wiki.

## Key Milestones

### 2023 — First correlational evidence ingested

**Chen et al. (2023)** — Observational study (n=120) demonstrating that spindle density during a post-learning nap predicts 24-hour recall of word-pair associations (r=0.43, d=0.81 for nap vs wake). Established the correlational foundation for the spindle-memory link in this wiki.

- Source: [[sources/chen-2023-sleep-memory]]
- Claim created: [[claims/claim-spindle-density-predicts-consolidation]] — initial confidence: **weak** (single observational study)

### 2024 — First causal evidence

**Rodriguez et al. (2024)** — Double-blind, sham-controlled RCT (n=84) showing that closed-loop auditory stimulation increased spindle density by 28% (d=0.95) and improved word-pair recall (d=0.68). Mediation analysis confirmed spindles accounted for 62% of the memory benefit.

- Source: [[sources/rodriguez-2024-spindle-stimulation]]
- Claim created: [[claims/claim-spindle-enhancement-improves-memory]] — confidence: **moderate**
- Existing claim upgraded: [[claims/claim-spindle-density-predicts-consolidation]] — weak → **moderate** (causal support added)

### 2025 — Failed replication challenges causal claim

**Kumar et al. (2025)** — Pre-registered, multi-site replication (n=204) across three labs. Spindle enhancement replicated (24%, d=0.88), but memory benefit did not (d=0.18, BF01=4.2 favoring null). Exploratory subgroup analysis revealed that only participants with low baseline spindle density benefited.

- Source: [[sources/kumar-2025-spindle-replication]]
- Claim downgraded: [[claims/claim-spindle-enhancement-improves-memory]] — moderate → **contested**
- Controversy created: [[controversies/controversy-spindle-stimulation-efficacy]]

## Evidence Trajectory

```
2023: Correlation observed (weak)
       ↓
2024: Causal RCT supports (moderate)
       ↓
2025: Multi-site replication fails for memory (contested)
       ↓  ↘
  [?] Meta-analysis needed    [?] Baseline moderator confirmation needed
```

## Current State (as of last update)

The spindle-memory field has moved from "promising but early" to "actively contested." The physiological effect of closed-loop stimulation is robust. The cognitive benefit is uncertain and may depend on individual differences. The field needs:

1. Confirmatory test of the baseline spindle density moderator hypothesis
2. Individual participant data meta-analysis
3. Studies in populations with known low spindle density (older adults, clinical populations)

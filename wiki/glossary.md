# Glossary

**Bayes factor (BF01)** — A ratio quantifying how much more likely the data are under the null hypothesis (H0) relative to the alternative (H1). BF01=4.2 means the data are 4.2 times more likely under the null. Conventionally, BF01 > 3 is "moderate evidence" for the null.

**Closed-loop stimulation** — A technique where sensory stimuli (e.g., auditory clicks) are delivered in real-time, timed to specific phases of ongoing brain oscillations. See [[methods/closed-loop-auditory-stimulation]].

**Mediation analysis** — A statistical technique testing whether the effect of X on Y is transmitted through an intermediate variable M. In the spindle context: does stimulation (X) improve memory (Y) by increasing spindles (M)?

**Memory consolidation** — The process by which initially labile memories become stable and resistant to interference, often occurring during sleep. See [[concepts/memory-consolidation]].

**Non-REM sleep** — Sleep stages 1-3 (N1, N2, N3), characterized by progressively slower brain oscillations. Spindles occur primarily in N2.

**Sleep spindles** — Bursts of 12-15 Hz oscillatory activity lasting 0.5-2 seconds, generated by thalamocortical circuits during non-REM sleep. See [[concepts/sleep-spindles]].

**Slow oscillations** — Large-amplitude ~0.75 Hz waves during deep (N3) sleep. Spindles often nest in the up-phase of slow oscillations.

**Spindle density** — Number of spindles per unit time (typically per minute or per 30-second epoch) of N2 sleep.

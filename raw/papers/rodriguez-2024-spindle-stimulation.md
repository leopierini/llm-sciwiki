# Auditory closed-loop stimulation enhances sleep spindles and declarative memory

## Abstract

While observational studies link sleep spindles to memory consolidation, causal evidence remains limited. We conducted a double-blind, sham-controlled randomized trial. 84 participants (age 22-40) learned word-pair associations, then slept overnight in the lab. The stimulation group (n=42) received precisely timed auditory clicks during the up-phase of slow oscillations to enhance spindle activity. The sham group (n=42) wore the same equipment with no stimulation.

## Results

Stimulation group showed 28% increase in spindle density compared to sham (p<0.001, d=0.95). Next-morning recall was significantly higher in the stimulation group (78% vs 66%, p=0.003, d=0.68). Mediation analysis confirmed spindle density increase mediated 62% of the memory benefit (indirect effect p=0.008).

No significant differences in sleep architecture (total sleep time, sleep efficiency, time in each stage) between groups, suggesting the effect was specific to spindle enhancement rather than general sleep improvement.

## Limitations

- Young adult sample
- One-night protocol — long-term effects unknown
- Word-pair task only
- Effect of repeated stimulation across multiple nights not studied
